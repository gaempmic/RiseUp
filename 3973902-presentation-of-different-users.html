Presentation of different users

What are the different roles available on the platform? What features are available for each role?
Written by Claire Philippe
Updated over a week ago

The roles available on the platform and described in this article are:

  • Administrator

  • Partner administrator

  • Designer

  • Animator

  • Community Manager

  • Group manager

  • Learner

For a summary of the different functionalities available by role, consult the following matrix: Simplified table of rights according to profiles

Administrator

The administrator is a user assigned the highest level of permissions on the Rise Up platform. Among their main assignments:

  • Pre-configuring the Rise Up platform (e.g. setting the platform’s colors, activating Catalogs, forums, configuration of the training registration mode, etc.).

  • Creating Partner Platforms.

  • Creating users, including registration of other administrators.

  • Creating training courses and training paths.

  • Registering users for training, training paths, etc.

  • Managing E-mails and legal documents.

  • Etc.

There can be several administrators, all with the same permissions. A minimum degree of coordination is needed between them. Administrators are the only people with the gear-wheel button at the top right of the top banner. This is the button is used to configure the platform.

Partner administrator

If it is decided to set up one or more partners, the administrator (of the parent platform) will be able to register one or more partner administrators, whose role will be to administer their own partner platform.

Partner administrators cannot configure their partner platforms (only administrators can to this).

As far as day-to-day administration is concerned (creating users, training, registering for training, etc.), apart from a few details, a partner administrator has the same permissions as an administrator (insofar as the administrator has given “full control” permissions to the partner administrators when creating partner platforms) but can only exercise these permissions over the scope of the partner users associated with their platform. It is possible to delegate some administrative permissions to partner administrators (for details, refer to the “Partner Platforms” module/stage).

Designer

The designer designs and creates training.

Designers have the necessary permissions to:

  • Create training and training paths (both on the main platform but also on partner platforms, where applicable),
    Please note that, by default, training created by a designer must first be approved by an administrator (maybe a partner administrator) to become accessible and visible to learners. However, it is possible to configure the platform to allow designers to publish training they have created directly, without having to go through approval by an administrator (or partner administrator).

  • Creating certificates (that the designer links to the training)

  • Viewing platform users, but they cannot create users (exclusive prerogative of the administrator or partner administrator).

  • Above all, designers can register users for the training and training paths they have created (or to all the trainings of the platform depending on your settings). They have access to the statistics of users and to do so, they have access to view all of the platform’s users.

Training may have several designers (co-designers), thus requiring a minimal degree of coordination between them.

The designer role applies across all platforms (parent and partner platforms) and all types (including public training or “anonymous” training).

You can configure the role of designer by choosing whether he can access all the training courses on the platform or only those for which he is the designer.

For more information, see the article Settings - Roles.

Facilitator

The facilitator is the person who facilitates training in a classroom or virtual class.

  • A facilitator from outside the company (facilitator role):

This is generally a user from outside to the company. For this reason, a facilitator role has been created; a person with this role cannot take a training course on the platform.

However, if the person has been appointed to facilitate blended training for the classroom (or virtual class) section, this person will be able to view the previous e-learning module forming part of the same training. A facilitator has access to the statistics for training where he or she facilitates a classroom module, etc. (including e-learning modules).

  • A facilitator from within the company (facilitator attribute):

It is possible to appoint a person within the company to facilitate a classroom session. In this case, you only need to designate any platform user as the session facilitator (this person will a role other than facilitator, most often simply a learner).

In this case, the person will have the same privileges as an external facilitator (facilitator role) over the sessions to be facilitated. In addition, this person will obviously have the permissions and access specific to their role (in general those of learner, but possibly any other role).

Community manager

The Community Manager’s role is limited to facilitating (moderating forums and creating discussion topics) forums and the news feed.

Group manager

When a user is created, one or more managers of this group can be created (they are then necessarily part of the group created).

Being a group manager entitles you to 2 basic functions:

  • Group manager(s) have access to the statistics for their group, considering all training combined.

  • Group manager(s) have permissions to approve registration requests (from the catalog) for training for their group members.

Furthermore, a group manager may be authorized to register group members directly for training. The administrator (partner administrator, as applicable) can really specify a restricted list of training courses for which a group manager can directly register their group members without there being a registration request from the learner group member (for details, refer to the “Managing Groups” module/stage).

You can configure the role of Group Manager by choosing whether he can create and modify users on the platform.

For more information, see the article Settings - Roles.

Learner

He/she learns and only has access to the statistics relating to him/her.

A learner is necessarily associated with only one Platform.

Either he/she is an “Internal” Learner if he/she has access to the Parent or Internal Platform;

Or he/she is a “Partner” Learner if he/she has access to one of the Partner Platforms.
