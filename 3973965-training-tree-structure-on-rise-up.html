Training tree structure on Rise Up

Training tree structure on Rise Up

Last updated on 07/24/2020
Written by Claire Philippe
Updated over a week ago

In summary:

  • Training is the central element of the Rise Up model.

  • All training comprises different modules (e-learning or classroom).

  • An e-learning module is made up of stages

  • A classroom module is made up of classroom sessions

  • It is possible to combine several training courses in a training path.

Training path

A training path therefore represents the highest level of the architecture.

A training path is a series (/a set) of several training courses that complement each other, the aim of which is to achieve a common learning objective.

To create a training path, you must have previously created all the training courses to be linked to it in the platform.

It is not essential to create and attach a “training path” to a training. Some training, which is the most conventional situation, comprises an individual training course, not included in a “training path”.

Below the training path is the training level.

Training

The aim of training is to acquire skills and knowledge. It consists of one or more modules.

Module

A module corresponds to a means of learning. There are actually two different kinds of module:

  • E-learning

  • Classroom (the definition of classroom also includes virtual classes)

Each module has its own structure, depending on whether it is an e-learning module or a classroom module.

  • An e-learning module is made up of stages. The stage corresponds to the content of the module, and forms the last level of an e-learning module. This content can vary in nature (video, documents, quizzes, surveys, etc.).
    Learners have to go through all the stages of an e-learning module to complete it.

  • A classroom module is made up of sessions. A session is defined by the date and place of the training session.
    Unlike the stages making up an e-learning module, all of which must be completed by the learner to complete the e-learning module, only one of the various classroom sessions offered in the module must be attended to complete the classroom module.
